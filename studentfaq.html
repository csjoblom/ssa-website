Where is the Jaqua Center Located?

The Jaqua Academic Center is located on the northeast part of the University of Oregon campus, across the street from Oregon Hall. The address is 1615 East 13th Avenue, Eugene, Oregon, 97403.

Where is the Jaqua IT Helpdesk Located?

The IT Area in the Jaqua Center is located on the third floor next to the elevator.

How do I check out a public tutor room?

Please see the receptionist at the front desk for information on scheduling use of a first floor tutor room.

Where do I go to get assistance with classroom accommodations and academic support for learning Differences?

Kate Wagner in JAQ 325 can help you with any issues and accommodations you might need.

Where do I check out laptops/adapters/chargers?

Between 8:00am and 5:00pm during the work week you can check them out at the IT Helpdesk located on the third floor. Nights/weekends you can check items out with Tony/Carolee in room 227 on the second floor.

Where do I turn in short term laptops/chargers/adapters?

Between 8:00am and 5:00pm you will return these items to the IT Helpdesk on the third floor. After 5:00pm or on the weekends you will return them to Tony or Carolee in room 227 on the second floor.

How do I rent a laptop for the year?

If you are eligible for laptop renta you must pay the $75.00 insurance fee to Shirley Brabham in room 225 in the Jaqua Center. After payment is cleared you will go to the IT Helpdesk on the third floor to fill out paperwork and recieve your laptop, case, and charger.

How do I rent a short-term laptop/charger/adapter?

To rent a short-term item you must bring a valid photo ID as collateral. During normal business hours checkouts will be done out of the IT Helpdesk. After hours and weekends they are handled in room 227 with Tony/Carolee.

How do I log into the lab computers?

Our computer labs are connected with campus authentication, so you will use your DuckID to log in. If for some reason you cannot log in, please use the username/password combination of student/student for guest access.

How do I print?

Our printing system is hooked into campus authentication as well. You will use your DuckID with the Papercut Client to print. If you are unable to log in using your duckid please see Chris Sjoblom in room 330 to get your account enabled.

How many pages can I print?

Student-Athletes recieve 800 pages per year. Tutors/FrontDesk/IT Staff recieve 200 prints per term. Black and white prints cost 1 page, color prints cost 10 pages, two-sided printing halves the cost.

How do I print two-sided?

Two sided printing is located under the dropdown menu "Layout" on the print menu in OSX. Select "Long-Edge binding" and be sure to print to one of the third floor printers.


How do I get windows on my Jaqua-issued laptop?

To get windows install please bring your laptop to the IT Helpdesk located on the third floor. The process can take up to 2 hours, so plan accordingly.

How do I request a tutor?

Tutor requests can be made on the Tutor Request Page.

Please see Chris Sjoblom in room 330.

What should I do if I spill liquid on my laptop?

If you spill any liquids on your laptop, immediately remove the power adapter and power down the device. You can quickly power down the device by pressing and holding the power button for 8 seconds. Be sure to report any accidents to the Jaqua IT Helpdesk immediately so we can help get you're laptop back up and running as quickly as possible.

What should I do if my laptop is non-functioning or damaged?

Please report any accidents to the Jaqua IT Helpdesk immediately so we can help get you're laptop back up and running as quickly as possible.

Who is eligible?
Services for Student Athletes provide advising and academic support services for any student listed in the most recent SSA student status report. For questions regarding eligibility contact SSA or sport advisor.
No Show Policy

Mandatory Attendance is required for tutor and structured study appointments! Students will be charged $10 per missed tutor session and $5 per missed structured study session for an UNEXCUSED reason.

***We review any missed academic appointment with the measurement of whether the reason for missing would be an excuse to miss practice or a competition. So students should consider if the reason for missing a tutor would work for missing practice.

***We understand things happen (i.e. car/home break in) that warrants consideration. We will handle every situation on a case-by-case basis.

Unexcused Absence
  • Student does not show up and there is no prior notice
  • Student shows up more than 10 minutes late
  • Student notifies tutor, SSA or advisor with or without adequate notice
Excused Absence
  • Student is traveling for competition
  • Student is sick or recovering form a medical procedure (We will need verification)
  • Family Emergency (We will need verification)
Late vs. No Show

Students are expected to be on time for all sessions. Students will be marked as late if they show up within the first 10 minutes (5 minute grace period). If students consistently show up late, a charge may be applied. If a student shows up more than 10 minutes late there is a one-time grace period of not being charged. Thereafter a charge will be applied for being grossly late and a no show will be noted.

Travel

If a student is too sick to attend a tutor appointment they must notify their tutor, advisor and see a trainer. If a student does not check in with a trainer/doctor the missed appointment will be deemed unexcused and student will be charged.

Rescheduled Appointments (Make up vs. Permanent)

Appointments should only be rescheduled (temporarily) if a tutor or student misses for an excused or unexcused reason. Students are not allowed to reschedule tutor appointments for their convenience. The “I don’t have anything to do” excuse does not warrant rescheduling an appointment. Students must still show up.

Permanent changes to tutor schedules should be addressed with the students advisor, not the tutor or tutor coordinator. Advisors must approve permanent changes to tutor schedules. Students are responsible for making the appointments on their schedule.

Sick

If a student is too sick to attend a tutor appointment they must notify their tutor, advisor and see a trainer. If a student does not check in with a trainer/doctor the missed appointment will be deemed unexcused and student will be charged.

Tutor Appointment Conduct

Students are expected to show up to all tutor appointments prepared with relevant books, notes, and handouts.

Students are expected to be productive, attentive and respectful during tutor sessions.

Cell Phones
  • Cell phones must be silent and stored during any session with a tutor, learning specialist, or advisor.
  • No text messaging for any purpose will be tolerated during any session with a tutor, learning specialist, or advisor. Tutors may enter a no show if student is continously texting during a session
